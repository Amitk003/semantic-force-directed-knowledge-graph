# Use Case

## Problem

Every day, people write down ideas, notes, research findings, and meeting summaries. But most note-taking apps treat each note as an isolated file in a folder. Over time, the collection grows into a chaotic pile of text. Finding connections between related ideas requires manual tagging, folder organization, or tedious full-text search. You never know what you are missing because the connections between your own ideas are hidden inside the text.

This is especially painful for:

- **Researchers** collecting papers and findings across related topics
- **Students** studying multiple subjects that share underlying concepts
- **Writers** weaving together ideas from different domains
- **Knowledge workers** who need to connect the dots across projects

## Solution

This app eliminates the manual organization burden. It uses on-device AI to read every note you add, understand its meaning, and automatically place it near other notes with similar ideas. The result is a living, visual map of your thinking.

Instead of asking "what folder did I put this in," you can look at the graph and see whole clusters of related ideas emerge on their own.

## Why It Matters

- **Zero manual work** - No tags, no folders, no linking by hand. Just write notes and watch the graph grow.
- **Discover hidden connections** - The AI surfaces relationships between notes you might never have linked yourself.
- **100% private** - Everything runs in your browser. Your thoughts never leave your machine.
- **Works offline** - After the first model load, no internet connection is needed.
- **Inclusive by design** - Falls back from WebGPU to WASM, so it works on nearly any modern device.

## Real-World Scenarios

- A student adding notes from different lectures sees how topics overlap
- A researcher imports paper summaries and finds unexpected thematic clusters
- A product manager collects user feedback and watches themes surface automatically
- A journaler reflects on past entries and sees how their thinking has evolved
